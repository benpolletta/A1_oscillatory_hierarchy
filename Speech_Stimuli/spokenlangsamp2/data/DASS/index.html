Digital Archive of Southern Speech

LDC2012S03

Introduction

Digital Archive of Southern Speech (DASS), Linguistic Data Consortium (LDC) catalog number LDC2012S03 and ISBN 1-58563-603-7, was developed by the University of Georgia. It is a subset of the Linguistic Atlas of the Gulf States (LAGS), which is in turn part of the Linguist Atlas Project (LAP). DASS contains approximately 370 hours of English speech data from 30 female speakers and 34 male speakers in .wav format and in .mp3 format, along with associated metadata about the speakers and the recordings and maps in .jpeg format relating to the recording locations.

LAP consists of a set of survey research projects about the words and pronunciation of everyday American English, the largest project of its kind in the United States. Interviews with thousands of native speakers across the country have been carried out since 1929. LAGS surveyed the everyday speech of Georgia, Tennessee, Florida, Alabama, Mississippi, Arkansas, Louisiana, and Texas in a series of 914 audio-taped interviews conducted from 1968-1983. Interviews average approximately six hours in length; the systematic LAGS tape archive amounts to 5500 hours of sound recordings. DASS is a collection of 64 interviews from LAGS selected to cover a range of speech across the region and to represent multiple education levels and ethnic backgrounds.

Data

The DASS speakers' average age is 61 years; there are 30 women and 34 men from the Gulf States region represented in this release. The interviews cover common topics such as family, the weather, household articles and activities, agriculture and social connections.

The interviews were originally recorded in the field on reel-to-reel audio tape. A digital version of every reel of tape was then made, one .wav file per reel, usually about one hour of sound. Each interview thus consists of a set of 3 to 13 reels, or roughly 3 to 13 interview hours. Personally identifying or sensitive information in the files was replaced with a tone to protect the privacy and to assure ethical treatment of speakers. Each .wav file is split into multiple .mp3 files based on the topic of conversation and labeled thusly. Included spreadsheets provide information about the speakers, the labels used for topics and the sound files.

Also included in this release is a version of the LICHEN software developed at the University of Oulu, Finland. LICHEN allows users to browse and search through the audio data in a more advanced fashion using a graphical interface. Further information and instructions for LICHEN can be found within the docs folder of this release. LICHEN requires VLC media player and java and runs on Windows. To launch LICHEN, run LAUNCH_LICHEN.bat located in the data/LICHEN/ folder.

In the event that LICHEN reports a file not found error when trying to play a particular audio file, this may be fixed by changing the file name on the hard drive to match the file name listed in LICHEN and within the error message.

Users who wish to use the data from the command line in *NIX systems may experience difficulty as many file names contain spaces and parenthesis. Those characters were left in the file names to ensure that LICHEN runs smoothly. Depending on your particular system, you may be able to utilize sed to overcome this. Inserting sed 's:\([ ()]\):\\\1:g' into your commands should produce escaped spaces and parenthesis and make mass processing feasible.

Directory Structure

Please see file.tbl for a complete file list as well as checksums for this publication.

  • data/
    • data/Biographies/ - Contains biographical information on each speaker.
    • data/DASS_Sound Files/ - Contains all of the audio files organized by speaker as well as notes on the recordings. The notes are of *meta.txt form.
    • data/LICHEN/ - Contains the LICHEN application. Running LAUNCH_LICHEN.bat starts the program.
    • data/Map Files - Contains the LICHEN application. Running LAUNCH_LICHEN.bat starts the program.
  • docs/ - Contains documentation on the corpus, LICHEN and spreadsheets with metadata on the speakers.
