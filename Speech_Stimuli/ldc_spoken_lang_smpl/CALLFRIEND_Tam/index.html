CALLFRIEND Tamil


Introduction

This directory contains sample data from CALLFRIEND Tamil, Linguistic Data Consortium (LDC) catalog number LDC96S59 and isbn 1-58563-074-8.

Tamil is a Dravidian language spoken in the Tamil Nadu state of Southern India where it is an official language, and in Sri Lanka where it is spoken by the Tamil ethnic minority. The language also has speakers in Singapore, Malaysia and South Africa. In all, some 66 million people use Tamil as their first language. It is a diglossic language with a literary version that is distinct from the everyday spoken dialect. While the written form of Tamil is fairly standard throughout its diaspora, there is no recognized spoken standard. However, such a standard form may be coalescing out of the upper cast Tamil dialects in Tamil Nadu.

Data

CALLFRIEND Tamil consists of 60 unscripted telephone conversations between native Tamil speakers lasting between 5-30 minutes each. The corpus also includes documentation describing speaker information (sex, age, education, callee telephone number) and call information (channel quality, number of speakers).

For each conversation, both the caller and callee were native speakers of Tamil; all calls were placed inside the continental United States and Canada.

Presented here are three two-minute samples from selected phone calls. The sample audio files are MS Wave files (.wav), but the actual corpus uses the NIST Sphere audio file format. Software to convert NIST to other formats is readily available through LDC. The demographic information listed below describes the persons placing the call in the sample files.
