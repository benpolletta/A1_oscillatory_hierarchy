CALLHOME Spanish

Speech and Transcript Sampler


Introduction

This file contains information about, and samples from, the CALLHOME Spanish collection, Linguistic Data Consortium (LDC) catalog numbers LDC96S35 (speech) and LDC96T17 (transcripts) and isbns 1-58563-083-7 and 1-58563-084-5, respectively.

CALLHOME Spanish was collected and transcribed by LDC primarily in support of the Large Vocabulary Conversational Speech Recognition (LVCSR) project sponsored by the U.S. Department of Defense. It consists of 120 unscripted telephone conversations between native Spanish speakers. The transcripts cover a contiguous 5 or 10 minute segment (see section 2 below) taken from a recorded conversation lasting up to 30 minutes.

Speakers were solicited by Rutgers University and LDC through personal contacts and via the internet. A total of 200 call originators were enrolled, each of whom placed a telephone call via a toll-free robot operator maintained originally by Rutgers University and later by LDC. Access to the robot operator was controlled by a unique Personal Identification Number (PIN) issued by the recruiting staff at Rutgers or LDC when the caller enrolled in the project. Each caller placed one telephone call and spoke up to 30 minutes.

All speakers were aware that they were being recorded. They were given no guidelines concerning conversation topics. Once a caller was recruited to participate in the collectoin, he/she was given a free choice of whom to call. Most participants called family members or close friends overseas. All calls originated in North America. The transcripts are timestamped by speaker turn for alignment with the speech signal and are provided in standard orthography.

In all, 200 calls were transcribed. Of these, 80 have been designated as training calls, 20 as development test calls, and 100 as evaluation test calls. For each of the training and development test calls, a contiguous 10-minute region was selected for transcription; for the evaluation test calls, a 5-minute region was transcribed. The data released by LDC (LDC97T17) contains only 20 of the evaluation test calls; the remaining 80 test calls are being held in reserve for future LVCSR benchmark tests.

Data

Presented here are three two-minute call samples and their associated transcripts. The audio files in the LDC corpus are in NIST Sphere format. The sample audio files are are in MS Wav format (.wav). Transcripts use the Western European ISO-8859-1 character encoding scheme. Transcriptions appear in both PDF and raw text to ensure compatibility.
