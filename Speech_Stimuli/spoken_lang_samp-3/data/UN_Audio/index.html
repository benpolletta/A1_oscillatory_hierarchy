United Nations Proceedings Speech

LDC2014S08

Introduction

United Nations Proceedings Speech, Linguistic Data Consortium (LDC) Catalog Number LDC2014S08 and ISBN 1-58563-693-2, was developed by United Nations and contains approximately 8,500 hours of recorded proceedings in Arabic, Chinese, English, French, Russian and Spanish. The data was recorded in 2009-2012 and comes from sessions 64-66 of the General Assembling (GA) and First Committee (FC), and meetings 6434-6763 of the Security Council.

The recording system used was called the NICE System, it was a customised recording system by NICE. Starting in July of 2013 the UN has moved to a new recording system called MAMS (Media Asset Management System) by SONY/Dalet. The recordings were done by following a daily internal circulated instruction from MMS (Meetings Management Section). Most of the subjects and related information of a particular meeting are published in a UN Journal which you can found in the following link: http://www.un.org/en/documents/journal.asp

Data

Data is presented either as mp3 or flac compressed wav and are 16-bit single channel files in either 22,050 or 8,000 Hz. It is organized by committee and session number, then language. The six UN languages are listed about and the folder labeled "Floor" indicates the mic used by the speaker at the time. These files may include other languages outside of the six.

File naming conventions for GA and FC data are in the form of LYY_ZZ_format.format and Security Council data is in the form of LYYYY_ZZ_format.format where L is a one letter language designation, YY is the meeting number, ZZ indicates the audio segment number and format.format is the wav or mp3 designation. Note that not all files are present for every language.
